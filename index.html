About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Statistics and Data Science, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), supervised by Prof. Zhixiang Lin. My current research focuses on integrating multi-omics with computational pathology for cancer diagnosis, tumor heterogeneity analysis, and interpretable multimodal modeling.

I received my M.Sc. degree in Statistics from Lanzhou University, and my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science and Technology from Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen, under the supervision of Prof. Yongbing Zhang.

During my Ph.D., I was supported by the China Scholarship Council (CSC) and conducted research at the Leibniz-Institut für Analytische Wissenschaften – ISAS – e.V., Germany, where I worked with Dr. Jianxu Chen. My doctoral research centered on computational pathology, including nuclei instance segmentation, multimodal large language models, and visual reasoning for cancer diagnosis.

I also maintain a collection of online Reading Notes and Figure Library, where I curate reading notes, key figures, and thematic summaries spanning computer vision, machine learning, multi-omics, and biomedical foundations.
